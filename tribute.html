Dr.Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives

Here's a timeline of Dr.Borlaug's life:

  • 1914 - Born in Cresco ,Iowa
  • 1933 - Leaves his family's farm to attend to university of Minnesota,thanks to a depression era program kanown as the "National Youth Administration"
  • 1935 - Has to stop school and save up more money.Works in the civilian conservation corps,helping starving Americans."I saw how food changed them,"he said ."All of these left scars on me"
  • 1937 - Finishes University and takes job in the US forestry service
  • 1938 - Marries wife at 69 years Margaret Gibson.Gets laid off due to budget cuts.Inspired by Elvis Charles,he returns to school study under Stakman who teaches him about breeding pest resistant plants.
  • 1941 - Tries to enroll in the millitary after the Pearl Harbour attack but is rejected. Instead the millitary asked his lab to work on waterproof glue,DDT to control malria,disinfectants and other applied science
  • 1942 - Receives a Ph.D. in Genetics and Plant Pathology
  • 1944 - Rejects a 100% salary increase from Dupont,leaves behind his pregnant wife and flies to Mexico to head a new plant pathology program.Over the next 16 years his team breeds 6000 different strains of disease resistant wheat including different varietes for each major climate on earth

"Borlaug's life and achievement are testimony to the far-reaching contribution that one man's towering intellect,persitence and scientific vision can make to human peace and progress"
